Consider the following two probability mass functions

\begin{subequations}
	\begin{equation}
		p = \gbrc{p_1,\ p_2},\quad \text{and}
	\end{equation}
	\begin{equation}
		q = \gbrc{q_1,\ q_2,\ q_3}.
	\end{equation}
\end{subequations}

Will the Shannon entropy $\func{\mathcal{S}}{p}$ always be smaller than the Shannon entropy $\func{\mathcal{S}}{q}$?
